Rainfall Depth Analysis

Objectives

This analysis aims to analyze the trends and statistics for rainfall depth in the Port Area of Metro Manila, Philippines, from 2000 to 2019 (20 years).

Key Findings

  • The highest annual rainfall depth occurred in 2012, while the lowest occurred in 2004.
  • The highest recorded monthly rainfall occurred in July 2002, showcasing a notable weather event.
  • Most of the monthly rainfall happens between May and October.
  • The majority of daily rainfall can be classified as heavy rainfall (50 mm to 100 mm).

Approach

  1. For data gathering, I obtained the rainfall data from PAGASA, the government agency responsible for weather monitoring in the Philippines.
  2. Python programming was used for data manipulation and data visualization.
  3. The missing values of the dataset was equated to zero.
  4. A bar plot was used to plot annual rainfall depth to showcase the difference of depth starting from 0 millimeters.

1. Annual Rainfall Depth

Plotting the annual rainfall depth can be helpful as it gives an overview of the rainfall depth data. It can show which years have a lot of rainfall and which do not. A bar plot is a typical plot used to visualize rainfall depth. The bar plot below shows that 2012 had the highest rainfall depth. On the other hand, 2004 had the lowest rainfall depth, with 2005 being close. The median is used in this analysis to prevent the effect of outliers. From the plot, the median value of the annual rainfall depth is about 2.10 millimeters. Another observation is that the lowest rainfall depth value is closer to the median than the highest rainfall depth value..

2. Monthly Rainfall Depth

A heatmap was used for the monthly rainfall depth, with the X and Y axes being the year and month, respectively. The color represents the rainfall depth, with a darker shade corresponding to a greater rainfall depth.

Based on the heatmap, one can see that the July of 2002 holds the darkest color which means that the month holds the highest monthly rainfall depth. Another observation from the plot is that the majority of the darker shades occur between May and October.

3. Daily Rainfall Depth

Plotting the daily rainfall depth shows that the values are erratic so there no trend to follow.

Classification (ChatGPT)

Another way to analyze daily rainfall data is to classify the rainfall depth into groups. The table below shows the classification of rainfall depth data according to ChatGPT. From the table, one can see that the highest rainfall count is from the "Heavy Rainfall" classification. Another observation is that there were 4,373 days without rainfall between 2000 to 2019.
