Electrical Consumption Analysis

Since I am coming from a background in civil engineering, designing utilities such as roads, and water supply is something I am familiar with. However, I have yet to explore a dataset involving electrical consumption. I wonder what insights I can gather from this data set from Maven Analytics.

1. Inspection of Data Set

Table 1.1 Number of Rows and Columns

Rows 52,416
Columns 9

Table 1.2 Field Names with Description and Data Type

Field Name Description Data Type
Datetime Date and time of recording Datetime
Temperature Temperature in Celsius Double
Humidity Concentration of water vapor in the air Double
WindSpeed Speed of the wind in kilometers per hour Double
GeneralDiffuseFlows Measurement of how much emissions gases diffuse into the broader atmosphere Double
DiffuseFlows Measurement of how much emissions gases diffuse into the local atmosphere Double
PowerConsumption_Zone1 Power consumption in Kilowatt-Hours in Power Zone 1 Double
PowerConsumption_Zone2 Power consumption in Kilowatt-Hours in Power Zone 2 Double
PowerConsumption_Zone3 Power consumption in Kilowatt-Hours in Power Zone 3 Double

From Table 1,2, we can see there are three zones included in this data set which are labeled Zone 1, Zone 2, and Zone 3. Additionally, there are other factors included in this time series data set such as temperature, humidity, wind speed, general diffuse flows, and diffuse flows. Due to my lack of knowledge regarding the effects of diffuse flows, I will omit it from my analysis.

2. Finding the Maximum and Minimum values in the Data Set.

Table 2.1 Minimum and Maximum Power Consumption with Range

Parameter Value Zone
Minimum Power Consumption 5,935 kWh Zone 3
Maximum Power Consumption 52,204 kWh Zone 1
Range 46,269 kWh

According to the table above, the zone that consumed the least electrical power is Zone 3 with a power consumption of 5,935 kWh. On the other hand, the most power consumed is 52,204 kWh and it was from Zone 1. Using the values of the maximum and minimum values, one can get the range of the values for the three zones which is 46,269 kWh.

3. Measures of Central Tendency

Table 3.1 Mean and Median of Power Consumption

Parameter Value
Mean Power Consumption 23,741 kWh
Median Power Consumption 22,678 kWh
Difference 1,603 kWh

Mean and Median are normally used to refer to the "middle point" of a dataset. One can see that the values of the two parameters are not equal with a difference of 1,603 kWh. With two unique values, which one will I use to refer to the middle point?

The mean of a dataset is obtained through the magnitudes of the values so it is affected by outliers. This explains why the mean is not a good indicator of the middle point of a data set if the data is skewed. Using a histogram can help in visualizing whether the data given is skewed or not.

From the distribution plot above, one can see that the data is skewed to the right. Following this observation, the mean is not a good reference point of the center of the dataset. In these situations, the median is much better fit for the center.
