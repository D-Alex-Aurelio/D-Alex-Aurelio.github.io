Electrical Consumption Analysis

The data set retrieved for this analysis was from the website of Maven Analytics. The data set has a total of 52,416 rows and 9 columns. The electrical consumption was obtained in intervals of ten minutes. Factors such as temperature, humidity, windspeed, and the gas emissions in the atmosphere.

Comparison of Power Zones

There are three zones considered in the electrical consumption data set. We shall label them Zone 1, Zone 2, and Zone 3. One parameter that we can use to compare the three zones is the distribution of power consumption values using a histogram.

From the histogram above, it is shown that Zone 1 has a lot of high power consumption values as compared to the other two zones. The next highest zone is Zone 2 followed by Zone 3. The distributions of the three zones are also different from each other. Zone 2 has one main peak around the 20,000 kWh mark with smaller peaks surrounding it. Zone 1 has a main peak around 32,500 kWh and another large peak around 22,500 kWh. Lastly, Zone 3 has a distribution that is skewed to the right with a rather tall peak compared to the other two zones.

Due to the skewed nature of the distribution of Zone 3, it is more applicable to use the median as the measure of central tendency. This is due to the mean being influenced by outliers as seen in the gap created between the mean and median in the plot of Zone 3. Another parameter to look into is that Zone 1 has the mean and median to be closest to each other despite having another large peak.
